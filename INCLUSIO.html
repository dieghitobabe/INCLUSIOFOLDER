About

Inclusio is a unique travel platform dedicated to highlighting culturally diverse destinations across the Lazio region in Italy. It aims to promote inclusion and cultural exchange by featuring locations and experiences that celebrate various global cultures.

Through Inclusio, travelers can explore a rich tapestry of traditions, cuisines, and histories, fostering mutual understanding and respect among different communities. This initiative encourages tourists to discover lesser-known gems that reflect the multicultural essence of Lazio, enhancing both local and visitor engagement with diverse cultural heritages.

Africa

The Africa Festival in Rome is a vibrant celebration of African culture, scheduled for March 10, 2024. Taking place at Via del Mandrione, 63, the festival features authentic African cuisine, music, dance performances, fashion shows, theater, and activities for children. Highlights include Senegalese dance by SUNU AFRICA, a fashion show by Zulfa Ramalheiro, a concert of lusophone music, Moroccan folk dances, and the theatrical performance "Women Crossing." The event runs from 12:00 PM to 9:00 PM. For more details, visit the official event page.

Russia

The Russian Center for Science and Culture in Rome, established in 2011, is located in the historic Palazzo Santacroce near Piazza Campo dei Fiori. The center aims to preserve the Russian-speaking community in Italy, strengthen Russia-Italy cultural and humanitarian ties, and support scientific and sports collaborations. It offers Russian language, history, and literature courses, hosts exhibitions, concerts, conferences, and film festivals, and includes a library and media center. For more information, visit their official site.

Morocco

The Great Mosque of Rome, located in the Parioli district, is one of Europe's largest mosques. Inaugurated in 1995, it spans 30,000 square meters and blends modern and traditional Islamic architecture. The mosque features a prominent minaret and a large prayer hall, accommodating thousands of worshippers. It also includes a cultural center, library, and educational facilities, promoting interfaith dialogue and understanding of Islamic culture. The Great Mosque is a symbol of religious tolerance and multiculturalism, reflecting Rome's rich history. Visitors can admire its architectural beauty and serene atmosphere.

Italy

Discover the hidden underground of Rome with a 3.5-hour guided tour of crypts and catacombs. Visit the Capuchin Crypt, decorated with the bones of 4,000 monks, explore ancient Roman catacombs, and see the Basilica of San Nicola in Carcere. The tour includes transportation, skip-the-line tickets, and expert guides. Suitable for those interested in history and archaeology, but not accessible for people with mobility issues. Starting point is Piazza Barberini. For more details, visit the GetYourGuide page.

China

The Chinese Magic Festival in Rome will be held on May 25-26, 2024, at Snodo Mandrione. This free event offers traditional Chinese food, magic shows, dance and music performances, martial arts demonstrations, embroidery exhibits, tea ceremonies, and various workshops, including cooking classes. The festival aims to immerse visitors in Chinese culture with activities for all ages. For more details, visit the official event page.
